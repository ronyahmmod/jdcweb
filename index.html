15th August is the day of national mourning. It’s a day when all beings of the whole Bengal, even nature drop tears. Because, this day of August, back in 1975 it seemed like this rainy August and brutal bloodshed of Bangabandhu were the representation of the weeping of the sky itself crying out of extreme grievance. The time when the murderers were brush firing all the family members of Bangabandhu at the hour just before dawn, it was raining, as if nature was weeping. Frightened Bangladesh against the raised firearms was all bemused in mourning and the unexpected sudden shock. This flame of bereavement will burn for epochs to come. 15th August is the day to recite lamentation, the 45th anniversary of the martyrdom of the architect of independence Father of the Nation Bangabandhu Sheikh Mujibur Rahman. Bangabandhu might have been killed physically but his soul is immortal. He lives eternally. Because he is the visionary and the architect of a Nation-state. As long as this country exists, he will exist immortally. He influenced the whole Bangali nation with the spirit of nationalism to be brave enough to start an armed revolution against the colonial ruler and oppressor of the Pak Military force. So he is immortal to the spirit of this nation. Bangabandhu is not just a person, the name of a great idea. The idea reinvigorated the whole nation. The bangali nationalism, democracy, socialism and secularism the philosophies with which the constitution of the country was created by the architect of independence and the greatest ever bangali in thousands of years, Bangabandhu Sheikh Mujib. In the realm of oppressors and the oppressed, Bangabandhu was beside the absorbed. In accordance with the continued 24 year-long struggles and movements against the oppressing Pakistani rulers, the call for independence he did in his great speech in the historic 7th March 1971 address at Sohrawardi Udyan is an unforgettable one. The call from his lightning voice, “This struggle, is our struggle for freedom. This struggle is the struggle for independence”, this immortal invocation mesmerized the whole oppressed nation of millions and made them start fighting for independence. Spellbound by that priceless call the nation of Bengal became the warriors without fear.

History of the college

A brief history Jibannagar Degree College was first established in 1984 at Upzila Head Quarter on 2.96 acre land. It got the recognition of the Board of Intermediate and Secondary Education, Jessore in the beginning year it started its academic activities in the session of 1984-1985. The center of H.S.C Exam was established in this college in 1988. The courses of B.A, B.S.S and B.B.S were opened in Degree level in 1992 and this courses were affiliated by National University in the session of 1993-1994. The Degree Examination center (pass) was first established in this college in 1999. Now 901 students are studying in H.S.C level and 446 students are in Degree Level. There are two Academic Buildings in this college. Two storeyed Academic Building is called Arts and Commerce Building and three storeyed building is called Science Building. All the academic activities are run these two buildings. This college has also an Administrative (one storeyed) Building having six rooms. There is a big library having various books which is run by a qualified Librarian. The activities of B.N.C.C and Rover Scouts are run by a qualified physical instructor in this college. The students take part in different sports all the year round. Very recently Governing Body (GB) of this college has decided to open the Honors Course in Bengali and Political Science. In the mean time teachers have been employed for Teaching in Honors level. For the permission of admission of the students in Bengali and Political Science and then affiliation, the college authority will apply to the National University as soon as possible.
